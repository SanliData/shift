# U.S. Energy Platform for Data Centers
## Investor Presentation (Final English Version)

## 1. Executive Summary
This project targets the rapidly expanding U.S. data center market by deploying an on-site power generation platform. The core value proposition is to acquire (or partner around) underutilized natural gas generation assets in Europe and Türkiye at distressed valuations and redeploy/integrate them to serve U.S. data center demand.

This approach is designed to deliver up to ~50% CAPEX advantage versus conventional greenfield buildouts.

Two generation units are currently identified as actionable opportunities under this project, and this report sets out the integrated implementation strategy.

## 2. Market Opportunity
U.S. data center investment is accelerating while power infrastructure expansion is lagging. In many regions, interconnection for large new loads is constrained by planning, permitting, and queue timelines that now extend over multiple years.

This is particularly visible in Texas and PJM (including Ohio and Virginia):
- According to ERCOT (Texas) projections, expected data center load reaches approximately ~78 GW by 2030. The proposed project scale is below 1% of this total.
- According to Oncor, active interconnection queue demand for data centers is approximately ~255 GW. The project represents approximately ~0.3% of that queue.
- In PJM, technical review for new interconnection requests typically takes 1–2 years, while full development and commissioning timelines are generally multi-year.

Conclusion: Data center demand is growing faster than grid connection capacity; in Texas and PJM, queue pressure makes on-site generation a strategic solution.

## 5. Contract and Licensing Structure
Gas Supply Agreements (7–12 Years)
- Henry Hub-indexed pricing with fixed margin and/or collar-band structures.
- Potential take-or-pay/minimum offtake obligations.
- Optional hedging to mitigate gas price volatility.

Data Center Power Sales Agreements (PPA – 10–15 Years)
- Fixed or indexed long-term pricing.
- Minimum capacity commitments in most cases.
- Integrated or behind-the-meter delivery structures.
